About Us

Hawaii Cat Cafe is a small cafe where you can socialize with adoptable rescue cats while enjoying a handcrafted beverage and European style pastries. All of the cats and kittens at the cafe are rescued by either our staff or the Humane Society.

“We are dedicated to changing the lives of sheltered cats by finding them forever homes, and educating the public about responsible pet ownership.”

Adopting cats from our cafe opens up space to rescue more cats. Together, we’re reducing euthanasia rates. Hawai‘i Cat Cafe allows potential adopters to get to know the cats’ personalities in a fun environment. Cats have significantly more exposure and opportunities to be adopted.

Prepare for Your Visit

Come hang out in Hawaii's first and favorite cat cafe! Play and cuddle with our adorable kitties from the Hawaiian Humane Society, and hopefully fall in love with one to take home!

Saving Your Spot

We highly recommend that you arrive 10 minutes early to read and sign the waiver. We do accept walk-ins, but we highly recommend that you make a reservation. We limit the amount of people in the Cat Lounge to ensure the safety and happiness of the cats.

COVID-19 Procedures

Masks/face coverings are required. You will be required to provide proof of vaccination along with a matching ID upon arrival for your visit. You must be fully vaccinated, which means that your last dose was received at least 2 weeks prior to your visit. Children under 12 years old are exempt. Refunds will not be issued for failure to provide proof of vaccination.

Age Restriction

Children 15 and under must be accompanied by an adult. No more than 2 children under 12 per one adult will be allowed in the Cat Lounge. Please contact us if you have any questions.

Parking

We have 3 reserved spots in the back of the building. If those are full, Kapahulu Avenue has lots of available metered street parking, and First Hawaiian Bank next to us has a pay to park option.

Best Time to Visit

Want a playful and fun interactive experience? Visit us when our cats are most active, either during our first sessionof the day at 11am, or our last session of the day at 5pm or 6pm.

Adopt a Cat

How to Adopt

To help you and the cats find a good match, potential adopters are required to meet and spend time getting to know the cats before applying to adopt. You can meet the cats by visiting during our regular hours. We recommend making a reservation, but we do accept walk-ins based on availability.

View Available Cats

All of the cats & kittens at the cafe are adoptable rescue cats. Click here to view available cats. If you are looking for a specific cat you saw on Hawaiian Humane Society website, but it is not in our album, that means it has already been adopted. We can not guarantee that any specific cat will still be available upon your visit, and we do not hold cats.

Health Details

All of the cats have received the following medical care:

  • Spay/Neuter
  • Microchip
  • Flea/parasite treatment
  • FIV/FeLV Negative
  • Vaccinated
Adopters will receive a copy of the medical records.

Questions

Can I bring my own cat to the cafe?

We’re sure you’re cat is great, and we’d love to see pictures of him/her, but for the health and safety of our cats, outside pets are not allowed.

I can't keep my cat anymore, will you take it?

Although we are partnered with a shelter, we are not an animal shelter ourselves. We can not take in animals off the streets or from individuals.

Can I take home one of the cats at the cafe?

YES!!! That’s what we’re here for! We do our best to review applications immediately so your new family member can go home with you that same day, but sometimes it can take up to 3 business days.

Are children allowed to visit the Cat Lounge?

Yes. We believe it is important for children to have the opportunity to spend time with animals and learn how to behave around them. You know your child best, and will be fully responsible for their behavior. Rules for respecting the cats will be strictly enforced and we will not hesitate to ask you and your child to leave without a refund if their behavior poses a threat to the cats. Children 15 and under must be accompanied by an adult. No more than two children under 12 per one adult. Please contact us if you have any questions.

Do you accept walk-ins for the Cat Lounge?

Yes, but we highly recommend that you make a reservation. We limit the amount of people in the Cat Lounge to ensure the safety and happiness of the cats.

Where are you located?

415 Kapahulu Avenue. We are located right next to the Texaco gas station.

Do you have parking?

Yes, we have 3 reserved spots in the back of the building. If those are full, Kapahulu Avenue has lots of available metered street parking, and First Hawaiian Bank next to us has a pay to park option.
